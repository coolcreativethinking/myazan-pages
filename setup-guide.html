My Azan Setup Guide

Get the Azan playing automatically on your Echo device at every prayer time, 5 times a day

Setting up automatic Azan takes 3 simple steps and only needs to be done once.

Takes about 5 minutes
You set your city → Connect Voice Monkey → Azan plays automatically
1

Open My Azan & Set Your City

Start by enabling the skill and telling it where you are.

Say to Alexa:
"Alexa, open My Azan"

Alexa will welcome you and ask for your city. Tell it your city:

Say:
"Set my city to Dubai"
(Replace "Dubai" with your actual city)

My Azan will automatically select the best prayer calculation method for your region (e.g., Dubai method for UAE, ISNA for North America, MWL for Europe).

Tip: If you're in a country with a specific method (like Egypt, Turkey, or Pakistan), the skill auto-detects it. You can also manually change it by saying "set calculation method to Egyptian".
2

Enable Automatic Azan

This is the key step that makes the Azan play at every prayer time without you having to open the skill.

Say to Alexa:
"Alexa, tell My Azan to enable automatic azan"

The skill will send a setup card to your Alexa app (on your phone). Open the Alexa app and find the card — it contains a link to complete setup.

In the Alexa App:
Home → scroll down to see the card from My Azan → tap the link
3

Connect Voice Monkey (One-Time Setup)

Voice Monkey is a free Alexa skill that allows My Azan to send announcements to your Echo device. Here's how to set it up:

3a. Install Voice Monkey on Alexa

  • Open the Alexa app on your phone
  • Go to More → Skills & Games
  • Search for "Voice Monkey"
  • Tap Enable (it's free)

3b. Create a Voice Monkey Account

  • Open your web browser and go to voicemonkey.io
  • Click Sign Up — use the same Amazon account linked to your Alexa
  • Once signed in, go to the Dashboard

3c. Get Your API Token

  • In the Voice Monkey dashboard, find API Token (shown at the top)
  • Copy it — it looks like vm-abc123def456...

3d. Create a Device (Monkey)

  • In the dashboard, go to Devices → Add Device
  • Give it a name like my-echo (no spaces, lowercase)
  • Link it to your Echo device when prompted

3e. Enter Your Credentials

  • Open the link from the Alexa app card (from Step 2)
  • Paste your API Token and Device Name
  • Click Activate Automatic Azan
Important: The device name must match exactly what you created in Voice Monkey (e.g., "my-echo"). It is case-sensitive.

You're All Set!

The Azan will now play automatically on your Echo device at every prayer time.

Our system checks prayer times every 5 minutes and sends the Azan announcement when it's time. You'll hear the beautiful call to prayer with a mosque image on Echo Show devices.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Voice Monkey free?

Yes! Voice Monkey has a free plan that covers everything needed for automatic Azan.

Do I need to keep the My Azan skill open?

No. Once set up, the Azan plays automatically even when you're not using the skill. Our system runs in the background and triggers announcements via Voice Monkey.

What if I move to a different city?

Just open My Azan and say "set my city to [new city]". The prayer times will update automatically.

Can I use it on multiple Echo devices?

Yes! Create multiple "monkey devices" in Voice Monkey (one per Echo) and contact us to link them to your account.

Why Voice Monkey and not Alexa Routines?

Alexa Routines require you to manually set each prayer time and update them monthly as times shift. Voice Monkey handles this automatically — prayer times are always accurate with zero maintenance.

Will it work during Ramadan?

Yes! During Ramadan, the skill automatically provides Suhoor and Iftar times, and the Azan includes relevant Ramadan features.

How do I stop the automatic Azan?

Say "Alexa, tell My Azan to disable automatic azan". You can re-enable it any time.

The prayer times seem off. What should I do?

Try changing the calculation method. Say "set method to Dubai" (for UAE), "set method to Egyptian" (for Egypt/Levant), "set method to ISNA" (for North America), etc. Different methods may differ by a few minutes.
